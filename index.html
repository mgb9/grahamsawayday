Small print — worth reading

Kick-off days aren't set yet. Leagues fix the weekend now but confirm Saturday-or-Sunday only later: Italy & Germany ~4–6 weeks ahead, Spain ~2 weeks, Portugal barely 10 days. For two-game weekends, book the trip around the weekend and pick your pair once days are announced. Weekends with 3–4 candidate games are the safest bets.

Tickets: buy only from official club shops (linked in our chat notes). Most open general sale 1–4 weeks before kick-off, no membership needed, except the marked hard tickets. Avoid viagogo-type resellers — clubs void those.

Scores: Atmosphere = the football occasion · Tickets = odds of face-value seats (the 🎟 line on each card says exactly where, when and for how much) · The Boss = sign-off odds — cost plus considerate timing while she's home with the kids · Value = flights + tickets + hotels combined · Graham = Wolves clash check — 5 wolves means Molineux is dark that weekend, 1 means it's derby day and he's not coming.

Beds and grounds: the 🏨 line is the going rate for a central double or twin (3★ hotel or Airbnb, per night, two sharing) booked a couple of months out on free cancellation — weekend surge pricing can beat it if you leave booking late. 📍 says where the stadium actually is and how to reach it from that base.

The nearly-missed list: the EU's biometric Entry/Exit System is live — allow extra time at passport control, especially for early flights. ETIAS (the €20 EU travel authorisation for UK citizens) launches around late 2026 and may be mandatory by April 2027 — apply only at the official EU site, travel-europe.europa.eu/etias; reminder set for 1 Feb 2027. Passports must be issued within 10 years and valid 3+ months beyond the return date — check now. Como, Benfica and Porto may be in European or domestic cup runs that can move league fixtures (17–18 Apr sits safely between the Champions League QFs and semis; late-April/May weekends carry more risk). The 06:15 Porto→Stansted flight needs a pre-booked ~€25 taxi (metro starts too late) and a 2-hour airport arrival. Italian match tickets are named per person — have every traveller's passport details ready and buy all seats in one transaction. And double-check the school's own 2026-27 calendar — academies sometimes differ from the county dates.

The Tuesday option (pending the boss): if a Sat–Tue window is approved, Monday-night fixtures become watchable rather than a scheduling risk, and returns get cheaper and kinder — flagged here so no option is scored on it until it's approved.

January and February flights are already on sale — winter-window trips can be booked at today's prices, unlike the spring options that wait on Ryanair's summer release. Winter caveats: kick-off days for Jan–Feb rounds get confirmed from ~Nov–Dec 2026, Wolves' FA Cup third-round fate (weekend of 9–10 Jan) is known at the December draw, and northern Europe in January is 0–6°C and damp — pack accordingly.

Flight times are current-season schedules — the routes are real but exact timings for spring 2027 firm up when each carrier publishes its season; the November flight reminder re-checks them. Graham scores assume Saturday Wolves fixtures — Sky moves Championship games to Friday/Sunday for TV, so his clash picture is final only when TV picks land ~6 weeks out.

No football anywhere on 27–28 March (international break) or, in Germany, on 1–2 May.
